## Buffering and Freezing Fixes

Buffering is the #1 support issue across all IPTV services. The good news: it's almost always fixable in minutes.

### Step 1 — Check Your Internet Speed
Run a speed test at [fast.com](https://fast.com). You need:
- 10 Mbps minimum for HD
- 20 Mbps for Full HD
- 25 Mbps for 4K

If your speed is below these thresholds, buffering is a network issue — not Apollo TV.

### Step 2 — Switch to Ethernet
Wi-Fi is the most common cause of IPTV buffering. Connecting your device via Ethernet cable eliminates wireless interference and provides a stable, consistent connection.

### Step 3 — Increase Buffer Size
In your IPTV app settings, increase the buffer size to 5–10 seconds. In IPTV Smarters:
- Go to Settings → Player Settings
- Set buffer time to 10 seconds
- Restart the app

### Step 4 — Lower Stream Quality
Temporarily switch from 4K to Full HD or HD to reduce bandwidth demand. Once your connection stabilizes, switch back.

### Step 5 — Restart Everything
In order: restart your router → restart your streaming device → reopen the IPTV app. This clears cached data that can cause freezing.

## Login and Credential Issues

### Credentials Not Received
If you subscribed but haven't received your username and password:
- Check your spam/junk folder
- Allow up to 10 minutes for delivery
- Contact WhatsApp support with your payment confirmation

### Wrong Credentials Error
Double-check for:
- Extra spaces before or after your username/password
- Capital letters — credentials are case-sensitive
- Correct portal URL — copy it exactly from your welcome email

### Expired Subscription
If your subscription has expired, your credentials will stop working. Contact Apollo TV support on WhatsApp to renew. Your same credentials are reactivated — no need to reconfigure your app.

### Subscription Active But Not Working
Occasionally a server restart is needed after renewal. Contact support on WhatsApp — this is typically resolved in under 5 minutes.

## Channel Not Loading

If specific channels aren't loading while others work fine:

- **Refresh your playlist** — in your IPTV app, pull to refresh or go to Settings and update the playlist
- **Try a different stream** — many channels have multiple streams (SD, HD, 4K). Try switching to an alternative stream
- **Check the channel category** — make sure you're browsing the correct category
- **Contact support with the channel name** — Apollo TV can push a fix for broken streams usually within 1 hour
